Over-Pumping Explained

Over-pumping occurs when water is extracted from an aquifer faster than it can naturally replenish, which can lead to dropped water tables, dry wells, land subsidence, and reduced water quality. It can also refer to temporary engineered pumping used during construction to divert sewage or water.

Dropped Water Tables

Excessive pumping can lower groundwater levels and dry out wells.
